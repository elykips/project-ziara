Portuguese Chapel

Did you Know?

Interesting & Useful facts
1 One of the oldest churches in East and Central Africa.
2 The chapel was built by Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama in 1498 while passing here on his way to India before St Francis Xavier visited Malindi in 1542 with his two sailors who died and were buried at the chapel in the same year, 1542.
3 Today there are many modern graves. Among them is: Grave of Malindi pioneer Commander Lawford of Lawford Hotel, J. Bell Smith, the first British administrator in Malindi who died in 1894, Charles Arnold Frank Mathews, buried in 1968. He was the son of Canon Mathew, a pioneer tea grower in Kericho and had gone to Malindi on holiday when he drowned while swimming.
4 The church has an altar with Catholic religious paraphernalia, two chairs and eight wooden benches on a cemented floor. It can accommodate 40 people seated and 50 to 60 standing.
5 The chapel is now St Francis Xavier Chapel and is under the Catholic Diocese of Malindi.
