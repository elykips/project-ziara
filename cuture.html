Maasai Mara

The Maasai tribe (or Masai) is a unique and popular tribe in Kenya due to their long preserved culture.

Did you Know?

Interesting and Useful facts
1 Maasai's distinctive culture, dress style and strategic territory along the game parks of Kenya and Tanzania have made them one of East Africa's most internationally famous tourist attractions.
2 The warrior is of great importance as a source of pride in the Maasai culture. Beginning life as a warrior means a young man can now settle down and start a family, acquire cattle and become a responsible elder.
3 Young Maasai girls are taught other functional roles like how to build houses, make beadwork, and cook and clean their homes, by their mothers and older women.
4 The Maasai tribe has a deep, almost sacred, relationship with cattle. They are guided by a strong belief that God created cattle especially for them and that they are the sole custodians of all the cattle on earth.
5 Women shave their heads and remove two middle teeth on the lower jaw (for oral delivery of traditional medicine). The Maasai often walk barefooted or wear simple sandals made of cow hide.
6 All of the Maasai's needs for food are met by their cattle. They eat the meat, drink the milk and, on occasion, drink the blood. Bulls, oxen and lambs are slaughtered for meat on special occasions and for ceremonies.
