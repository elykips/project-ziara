Old Town Mombasa

Did you Know?

Interesting & Useful facts
1 Has houses, shops, markets and Mandry mosque which was built in 1570.
2 At the entrance of the old town is a coffee pot and cup, a gift to Mombasa which was built by Mr. Burhan Ali Taher in 1988. Next, to the coffee pot, there is a remnant of the original trolley track which was laid in 1890.
3 Another of these buildings is Old post office, a good example of Mombasa architecture with covered balconies on the front and back sides supported by wooden brackets.
4 The Old Port was a major part of the triangular dhow trade of the Indian Ocean and the Arabian Gulf. Burton, the explorer, described how hundreds of ships could be seen around the port.
5 The old law courts opened in 1902 by the British colonial governor, Sir Charles Eliot are also present.
6 The Mazrui Graveyard, belonging to the Mazrui Family who ruled Mombasa in the 18th century is another landmark.
7 The Africa Hotel opened in 1901.
