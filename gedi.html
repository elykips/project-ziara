Gedi Ruins

Did you Know?

Interesting & Useful facts
1 Gede was founded in the 12th Century AD. Its name, from the Ormaic Gede, alludes to the abundant water of its deep stone wells.
2 Excavations unearthed the ruins of the big mosque, the royal house, and the royal tombs. Currently, the Monument is under the care of the National Museums of Kenya.
3 Excavations between 1948 and 1958 revealed that the Muslim inhabitants traded with people from all over the world. Some of the findings included beads from Venice, coins and a Ming vase from China, an iron lamp from India, and scissors from Spain.
4 Gede had a mosque, a palace, and large stone houses. These houses were complex for their time, with bathrooms that had drains and overhead basins to flush toilets.
5 The Oromo word "Gede" means "precious", but the town might have been named after the last Oromo leader to camp on the site.
6 Gede's eventual abandonment to nature is believed to be as a result of a number of factors including the removal of the Sheikh of Malindi and the Portuguese to Mombasa in 1593.
