Shimoni Caves

Did you Know?

Interesting & Useful facts
1 Shimoni translates as ‘ place of the hole’ in Swahili.
2 Shimoni caves extend inland for up to 20 kilometers and are held sacred by local Digo people.
3 During the slave trading era, the caves were used to keep captives from the interior before they could be shipped for sale at the Zanzibar slave market.
4 Inside the caves, there are old iron shackles, wooden crates, rusted chains, and metal studs embedded into the walls, as well as the natural well of semi-saline water from which slaves were forced to drink.
5 There is a song about Shimoni Caves, sang in 1983 by Roger Whittaker, a Kenya- born singer and songwriter.
